History of Tembleque

Tembleque is a traditional Puerto Rican dessert with a rich cultural history rooted in the island's use of coconut and Spanish culinary influences. The name "tembleque" means "tremble" in Spanish, which perfectly describes the dessert's jiggly, custard-like texture. Often served during the holiday season, tembleque is made primarily from coconut milk, cornstarch, sugar, and a touch of cinnamon, offering a creamy, slightly sweet, and fragrant treat that resonates with the island's tropical flavors. This dessert also shares a lineage with other Latin American coconut-based treats, such as the Filipino maja blanca and Spanish manjar blanco, but remains uniquely Puerto Rican.

Ingredient List

  • 2 (14 ounce) cans coconut milk
  • ¾ cup sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • ⅓ cup 2% milk
  • 1 pinch ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. Stir together coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a saucepan. Spoon 3 to 4 tablespoons coconut milk mixture into a small bowl and stir in cornstarch until dissolved; pour into the saucepan. Bring mixture to a boil while stirring constantly; cook until smooth and thick, about 5 minutes.
  2. Pour into molds, cover each with plastic wrap, and refrigerate until cold and firm, 3 hours to 2 days.
  3. Run a thin knife around the edges of each mold; invert onto serving plates. Garnish tops with cinnamon.
